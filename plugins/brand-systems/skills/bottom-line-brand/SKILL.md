# Bottom Line Brand Guidelines Skill

## Overview
This skill provides comprehensive brand guidelines for Bottom Line, an educational organization focused on helping underserved college students succeed. Use this skill whenever creating ANY branded content - documents, presentations, PDFs, images, web content, or other materials.

## Brand Identity

### Mission & Voice
Bottom Line's tagline is "Get in. Graduate. Go far." The brand embodies:
- **Strength and ambition** - Bold, collegiate-inspired design
- **Approachability** - Welcoming, supportive tone
- **Energy and optimism** - Dynamic, electric, forward-moving
- **Education focus** - Supporting students from access through career success

### Core Programs
- **Access** (Red: #D73724) - Getting high school students into college
- **Success** (Violet: #A632AE) - Supporting students through graduation
- **Blueprint** (Blue Violet: #39319D) - AI-powered advising program
- **Learning Labs** (Green: #00775B) - Educational support initiatives

## TYPOGRAPHY

### Font Hierarchy

#### Primary Font: Crimson Pro
- **Usage:** Primary headers, secondary headers, large statements
- **Characteristics:** Serif, professional, collegiate feel
- **Fallback:** Georgia

#### Secondary Font: Roboto
- **Usage:** Body copy, long-form text, small text, captions
- **Characteristics:** Sans-serif, clean, readable
- **Fallback:** Arial

#### Specialty Font: Saira Extra Condensed
- **Usage:** Short headlines, large callouts, numerical treatments
- **Characteristics:** Bold, condensed, impactful, uppercase recommended
- **Fallback:** Impact

### Typography Rules

1. **General Typesetting:**
   - Use strategic contrast in size, weight, and color
   - Maintain alignment and consistency
   - Keep content easy to read, understand, and visually pleasing
   - Typography should guide reader's attention and enhance storytelling

2. **Headline Guidelines:**
   - Can be set in either Saira Extra Condensed OR Crimson Pro
   - Saira Extra Condensed: Short, impactful headlines (avoid for very long headlines as it's less effective in extended text)
   - Crimson Pro: Versatile for various headline lengths

3. **Body Copy:**
   - Set in Roboto
   - Maintain proper line spacing for readability

4. **Example Hierarchy:**
   - H1: Saira Extra Condensed, Uppercase (or Crimson Pro)
   - H2: Crimson Pro
   - H3: Crimson Pro (smaller)
   - Body: Roboto
   - Intro text: Roboto (larger)
   - CTA: Saira Extra Condensed, Uppercase

### Fallback Fonts
When brand fonts unavailable:
- **Saira Extra Condensed** → Impact
- **Crimson Pro** → Georgia
- **Roboto** → Arial

Apply all other brand rules when using fallback fonts.

**Program Backgrounds (for program-specific materials):**
7. **Red background** → Yellow logo

10. **Violet background** → White logo
11. **Blue Violet background** → Yellow logo
12. **Blue Violet background** → White logo
13. **Green background** → Yellow logo
14. **Green background** → White logo

#### Logo Usage Rules:
- **Clear space:** Minimum equivalent to height of logo must be observed around all sides
- **Minimum size (print):** 1.0 inch wide
- **Minimum size (web):** 75 pixels wide
- **Placement:** Centered (primary) or corner placement if layout allows left-alignment with text
- **Text setting:** Set name as "Bottom Line" in title case with space between words
- **Tagline:** NEVER remove "Get in. Graduate. Go far." from logo (exception: apparel/swag where tagline will be illegible)

#### Logo Restrictions - NEVER:
- ✗ Change logo colors (only approved combinations)
- ✗ Add or delete elements
- ✗ Place logo at an angle
- ✗ Stretch the logo
- ✗ Add bevels, drop shadows, or effects
- ✗ Crop the logo
- ✗ Place logo on complex imagery
- ✗ Place logo in white box when using image backgrounds
- ✗ Add space within the logo
- ✗ Recreate the typeface
- ✗ Outline the logo
- ✗ Add drop shadow or filter effects
- ✗ Place logo in bounding shape (other than yellow rectangle)

#### Logo on Photography:
- **Dark images:** Use yellow or white logo
- **Light images:** Use black logo
- Ensure image is uncluttered and mark is clearly legible

## THE BAR (BOTTOM LINE)

### Bar Specifications
- **Visual Element:** Yellow horizontal bar with text/URL
- **Purpose:** Foundational element for holding URLs and key concepts
- **Usage areas:** Brochure/presentation covers, advertisements, swag

### Bar Guidelines

#### Placement:
- Center at bottom of composition
- Ensure consistent margins on left, right, and bottom

#### Size:
- Should remain thin (approximately 0.25 inches high in 8.5 x 11 layout)
- Preserves appearance as a line
- Avoid thick bar or color block look

#### Color:
- **Keep yellow** in all compositions
- If background is already yellow, use **white**

#### Text Within:
- Designed to highlight important text (ideal for URLs as CTAs)
- Can house subheads, titles, report details
- Maintains focus and clarity

### Bar Usage Examples:
- Presentation covers
- Brochure covers
- Advertisement layouts
- Material designs

## ICONOGRAPHY & GRAPHIC VISUALIZATIONS

### Icon System

#### Icon Design Philosophy:
- **Simple:** Universally understood symbols
- **Clarity:** Recognition across diverse audiences
- **Consistent:** Aligned with brand messaging
- **Functional:** Quick story conveyance, clarity to messaging

#### Available Icons (8 core icons):
1. **Gears** - Process, systems, mechanics
2. **Mobile device** - Technology, connectivity, digital
3. **List/lines** - Organization, planning, steps
4. **Growth chart** - Progress, achievement, metrics
5. **Compass** - Direction, guidance, navigation
6. **Lightbulb** - Ideas, innovation, insight
7. **Connection nodes** - Network, relationships, collaboration
8. **Chat bubble** - Communication, dialogue, support

#### Icon Color Treatments:

**Primary Colors:**
- Yellow background: Use primary colors (black/white/teal)
- White background: Use black and teal
- Black background: Use yellow and white icons
- Teal background: Use yellow and white icons

**Program Colors:**
- Red background: Use yellow, red icons (white/black as needed)
- Violet background: Use yellow, violet icons
- Blue Violet background: Use yellow, blue violet icons
- Green background: Use yellow, green icons (use teal icons with color blocks)

#### Icon Usage Guidelines:
- Select icons that complement message/theme
- Keep simple and scalable (clear even at small sizes)
- Use with universal meanings
- Use solid shapes with heavy lines
- Avoid excessive detail
- Integrate subtly - place near relevant text for emphasis/guidance
- Can accent entire block of text OR use multiple icons together for bulleted lists

## DESIGN BEST PRACTICES

### Document Creation

#### For Word Documents (.docx):
1. Set page size and margins
2. Apply Roboto for body (11-12pt)
3. Apply Crimson Pro for headings (16-24pt)
4. Use yellow sparingly for highlights/callouts
5. Use black for primary text on white
6. Include logo (centered or corner) with proper clear space
7. Add "The Bar" at bottom for URLs/key info if appropriate

#### For Presentations (.pptx):
1. Use title slides with program colors or teal backgrounds
2. Apply Saira Extra Condensed for slide titles (uppercase)
3. Use Roboto for body content
4. Limit text per slide (use visuals)
5. Incorporate icons to support key points
6. Use data visualizations with proper color pairings
7. Include logo on title slide and monogram on subsequent slides
8. End with "The Bar" containing CTA/URL

#### For PDFs:
1. Follow document/presentation guidelines above
2. Ensure all fonts are embedded
3. Verify color accuracy (use provided color codes)
4. Check that logos maintain clarity
5. Test accessibility/readability

#### For Images/Graphics:
1. Use bold color blocks (yellow, teal, program colors)
2. Incorporate monogram for brand recognition
3. Apply typography hierarchy
4. Use icons to support messaging
5. Ensure sufficient contrast for text readability
6. Follow color pairing guidelines

### Layout Principles

1. **Hierarchy:** Guide reader's eye with size, weight, color
2. **White space:** Don't overcrowd - let content breathe
3. **Alignment:** Keep elements organized and professional
4. **Consistency:** Use same fonts, colors, spacing throughout
5. **Balance:** Distribute visual weight across composition

### Accessibility

1. **Color contrast:** Follow provided accessible pairings
2. **Font size:** Minimum 11pt for body copy
3. **Alt text:** Include for all images/icons
4. **Clear language:** Write concisely and clearly
5. **Structure:** Use proper heading hierarchy

## SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

### Co-Branding with Partners:

When creating materials with external partners or collaborators:

1. **Primary branding:** Bottom Line brand takes precedence on program-facing and student-facing materials
2. **Co-branding:** Partner logos may appear alongside the Bottom Line logo when appropriate, but must not compete visually — Bottom Line logo should be equal or larger
3. **Program alignment:** Use program colors (Red/Violet/Blue Violet/Green) for specific initiative materials
4. **Student-facing content:** Always follow full Bottom Line brand guidelines
5. **Internal reports:** Can adapt brand while maintaining core elements (colors, fonts)

### Program-Specific Design Tips:

When creating materials for specific programs:
- **Access (Red):** Energetic, action-oriented — use bold headlines, growth charts, and compass icons to convey direction and opportunity
- **Success (Violet):** Supportive, celebratory — use connection nodes and chat bubble icons to convey community and guidance
- **Blueprint (Blue Violet):** Innovation-focused — use lightbulb, gears, and mobile device icons to convey technology and forward thinking
- **Learning Labs (Green):** Knowledge-driven — use list/lines and growth chart icons to convey structure and progress
