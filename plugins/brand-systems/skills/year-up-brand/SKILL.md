# Year Up United Brand Guidelines Skill

Apply Year Up United's current visual identity and brand voice consistently across all deliverables. This skill reflects the YUU brand portal as of mid-2026.

## When to Use This Skill

Use whenever:
- Creating presentations, documents, reports, spreadsheets, or web content for Year Up United
- Building student-facing materials for Career Connect or any other YUU program
- Writing or editing any text that represents YUU or a YUU sub-brand
- Formatting existing materials to match the YUU brand
- User mentions YUU, Year Up United, Career Connect, or asks for "on-brand" formatting
- Building HTML artifacts, landing pages, or digital assets for YUU
- Applying the arrow graphic system, sub-brand lockups, or color combinations

## Part 1: Brand Identity & Mission

### About Year Up United

Year Up United is a workforce development nonprofit founded in 2000 that empowers young adults and connects them with meaningful career opportunities. The organization builds pathways to meaningful careers for untapped talent, helps companies become more equitable employers, and collaborates with like-minded organizations to close the Opportunity Divide at scale.

**Key stats**: 50K+ participants served, 1,500+ employer partners, 30% average wage gain at six years post-graduation, 2,700+ talent placements via YUPRO Placement.

### Sub-brands & Service Brands

YUU's identity system places sub-brands on a "restrained ↔ expressive" spectrum:

- **Grads of Life** — most restrained (corporate-facing thought leadership)
- **YUPRO Placement** — restrained (talent placement arm; "Part of Year Up United" endorsement)
- **Year Up United** (core) — central
- **Career Pathways** — central (the flagship full-time, hybrid, year-long program)
- **Career Connect** — central (6-week virtual career readiness program for college students)
- **Talent Networks** — expressive (employer partner network umbrella)
- **Alumni** — most expressive

**Important distinctions:**
- **Career Connect ≠ Career Pathways.** Career Connect is 100% virtual, 6 weeks, for college students. Career Pathways is full-time, hybrid, year-long. Student-facing materials that could be confused should include a disambiguation note.
- All sub-brands use the same YUU color palette — there is no separate "Career Connect blue" or "Career Labs blue." Older materials may show this; do not propagate it.

### Brand Voice & Tone

YUU's voice is:

- **Empowering and confident**: Students are capable professionals building their futures, not charity recipients. "Step confidently into your next chapter."
- **Community-oriented**: Belonging, peer support, cohort experience. "Everyone belongs."
- **Action-oriented**: Fast, friendly, and direct. "The more you put in, the more you'll get out."
- **Warm but professional**: Approachable without being casual. The organization bridges young adults into professional environments — the tone models that bridge.
- **Motivational**: Growth mindset framing. Reframe anxiety as excitement.

**Key phrases and patterns**:
- "Close the Opportunity Divide"
- "Skills-first approach" / "skills, not just degrees"
- "Untapped talent"
- "Step confidently into your next chapter"
- "Practice SLANT" (student engagement framework)

### Official Naming

- **Full name**: "Year Up United" (first reference, formal contexts)
- **Short name**: "YUU" (internal only — avoid in student-facing materials)
- **Career Connect**: "Year Up United Career Connect" (first reference) → "Career Connect" (subsequent)
- **Website**: www.yearupunited.org
- **Career Connect email**: careerconnect@yearupunited.org

## Part 2: Visual Brand Identity

### Color Palette

YUU uses a two-tier color system: **neutrals for text/structure** and **accents for graphics/emphasis**.

#### Neutral Colors (primary use: headlines, body text, backgrounds)

| Name | HEX | RGB | CMYK |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Navy** | `#170055` | 23, 0, 85 | 89, 98, 0, 35 |
| **Digital Gray** | `#EEEEEE` | 238, 238, 238 | 10, 7, 5, 0 |
| **Black** | `#000000` | 0, 0, 0 | 0, 0, 0, 100 |
| **White** | `#FFFFFF` | 255, 255, 255 | 0, 0, 0, 0 |

Note: Digital Gray is for digital/web only. Use White as the print specification.

#### Accent Colors (graphics + large headlines only — NOT body text)

| Name | HEX | RGB | CMYK | PMS |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| **Purple** | `#B4A1FF` | 180, 161, 255 | 33, 42, 0, 0 | 2645 C |
| **Yellow** | `#E4F221` | 228, 242, 33 | 16, 0, 95, 0 | 387 C |
| **Blue** | `#008AFF` | 0, 138, 255 | 75, 26, 0, 0 | 2925 C |
| **Orange** | `#FE6500` | 254, 101, 0 | 0, 75, 100, 0 | 021 C |
| **Red** | `#FF1D46` | 255, 29, 70 | 0, 95, 67, 0 | 1787 C |
| **Pink** | `#FF5192` | 255, 81, 148 | 0, 83, 10, 0 | 212 C |

**Rule**: Accent colors are always paired with neutrals. Never combine two accents in the same composition (no purple + yellow text, no orange + pink graphics).

### Color Accessibility

Three contrast tiers determine where each color combination can be used. Always pick the highest tier that works for your content.

- **AAA (pass 7+)** — All text formats, all sizes. Highest accessibility.
- **AA (pass 4.5+)** — Body and headlines OK.
- **3+** — Large headlines and bolded text only (**18pt+ minimum**). Never for body text.

#### Tier 1 (preferred for all text)
- Navy `#170055` text on White or Digital Gray
- Black `#000000` text on White, Digital Gray, Purple, or Yellow
- White or Digital Gray text on Navy or Black
- Yellow or Purple text on Navy or Black

#### Tier 2 (large headlines OK)
- Navy text on Orange, Pink, Blue, Red
- White text on Pink, Blue, Red

#### 3+ (large bold headlines only, ≥18pt)
- Red, Blue, or Pink text on White, Digital Gray, or Yellow
- White, Digital Gray, or Yellow text on Red, Blue, or Pink

#### Color Misuse — Do NOT
- Use white text on backgrounds that don't at least meet 3+ contrast
- Use Yellow, Purple, or Orange accent text on White or Digital Gray (fails contrast)
- Use 3+ combinations for body or text less than 18pt bold
- Combine two accent colors in the same composition

### Typography

YUU has two typeface systems depending on who's producing the work.

#### Primary typefaces (licensed — designers / external creative vendors only)

- **Nimbus Sans Extended** — headlines, large intro paragraphs, numbers, dates, infographics. Expressive, modern, clean, confident.
- **Bookmania** — body copy, captions, labels, page headers, footers. Friendly serif, evokes trust.

#### Global Microsoft / Web fonts (Word, PowerPoint, email, internal staff — what most people use)

- **Verdana Bold** — title text, headlines, sub-headers, stats/metrics, emphasized text
- **Times New Roman** — standard body copy

This is what every Word doc, PPT, email, and Microsoft-stack deliverable produced internally should use. Do **not** substitute Calibri, Arial, Helvetica, Montserrat, or other fonts — Verdana + Times New Roman is the spec.

#### Font usage pattern

- **Headlines/titles**: Verdana Bold, large size, Navy `#170055` on light backgrounds or White on dark
- **Sub-headers**: Verdana Bold, medium size
- **Body text**: Times New Roman Regular, Navy on White (or White on Navy/Black)
- **Captions/footers**: Verdana or Times New Roman, smaller size

### Logo

YUU's primary logo is a lockup: the stylized orange "Y" mark + "Year Up United" wordmark.

#### Logo variations (in `assets/`)

| File | Description | Use on |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| `yuu-logo-primary.png` | Orange Y + Black wordmark | White / light backgrounds |
| `yuu-logo-white.png` | Orange Y + White wordmark | Navy / dark backgrounds |
| `yuu-logo-black.png` | All-black (secondary) | Light backgrounds, single-color |
| `yuu-logo-white-only.png` | All-white (secondary) | Dark backgrounds, single-color |
| `yuu-y-mark-orange.png` | Standalone orange "Y" symbol | Icons, favicons, small spaces, watermarks |
| `yuu-y-mark-navy.png` | Standalone navy Y | Single-color contexts |
| `yuu-y-mark-white.png` | Standalone white Y | Dark backgrounds |
| `yuu-y-mark-black.png` | Standalone black Y | Light backgrounds |

The full-color logos (Orange + Black, Orange + White) are **primary**. The Y in the full-color version is always Orange `#FE6500`. The all-white and all-black versions are acceptable secondary options.

#### Clear space
Maintain clear space equal to the height of the "Y" in the wordmark on all sides. Never crowd the logo against edges or other elements.

#### Minimum size
- Full lockup: 1" wide print / 144px wide digital
- Y symbol alone: 0.1875" tall print / 16px tall digital (used for favicons, social avatars)

#### Logo don'ts
- Do **not** skew, compress, or stretch the logo
- Do **not** change any colors (the Y stays Orange in the full-color version)
- Do **not** add drop shadows, glows, or effects
- Do **not** place on busy backgrounds without sufficient contrast
- Do **not** create new lockups (no "Year Up United | Career Pathways" style lockups outside the approved sub-brand set)
- Do **not** hold the logo inside a containing shape (no rounded boxes around it)

### Graphic Elements: The Arrow System

YUU's signature graphic element is the **arrow system** — abstract, dynamic arrows that represent the many directions a career trajectory can take. These replace the older orange rectangular border motif used in pre-2026 materials.

#### Available arrow colors (in `assets/`)

| File | Color |
| :---- | :---- |
| `arrow-navy.png` | Navy `#170055` |
| `arrow-orange.png` | Orange `#FE6500` |
| `arrow-yellow.png` | Yellow `#E4F221` |
| `arrow-blue.png` | Blue `#008AFF` |
| `arrow-red.png` | Red `#FF1D46` |
| `arrow-pink.png` | Pink `#FF5192` |
| `arrow-purple.png` | Purple `#B4A1FF` |

#### Arrow usage patterns

- **Supergraphic** — Large arrow, foreground element, loud and proud
- **Outlined** — Thin outline version, restrained / corporate
- **Cropped** — Used as background or photo crop, with text overlaid
- **Pattern** — Multiple arrows combined to create patterns

#### Arrow rules — Do
- Always use a single solid color (no gradients)
- Always pair with a neutral (Navy + arrow accent, or White + arrow accent)
- Use beside text sections or in place of bullets

#### Arrow rules — Do NOT
- Use arrows as dividers between two text sections (they're not separators)
- Use arrow outlines with small overlaid text (legibility)
- Use thick outlines (only thin outline style)
- Place colored arrows on colored backgrounds (one or the other must be neutral)
- Add drop shadows or effects
- Layer arrows on top of each other
- Stretch or distort arrows

### Sub-Brand Logo Lockups

Each YUU sub-brand has a standardized lockup with the YUU logo + sub-brand wordmark, separated by a vertical line divider with clear space equal to the width of two letter "t"s.

#### Sub-brand options
- **YUU Alumni** — `[YUU logo] | Alumni`
- **YUU Career Pathways** — `[YUU logo] | Career Pathways`
- **YUU Talent Networks** — `[YUU logo] | Talent Networks`
- **YUPRO Placement** — standalone wordmark with "Part of Year Up United" tagline
- **Grads of Life** — standalone wordmark, can be paired with YUU on the same layout (not as a lockup)

**Do not create new sub-brand lockups.** The set above is fixed. "Career Connect" does not have its own lockup — it uses the standard YUU logo.

### Photography Style

- **Subjects**: Diverse young adults (18–29) in business professional or polished business casual attire
- **Settings**: Corporate offices, urban outdoor environments, meeting rooms, group settings
- **Mood**: Confident, professional, community-oriented, aspirational
- **Diversity**: Reflect the broad demographic of YUU participants
- **Treatment**: Photos can be cropped using arrow shapes as a graphic device

### Testimonial / Quote Treatment

When pulling quotes (e.g., participant testimonials):
- Quote text in Navy bold for emphasis phrases
- Attribution in smaller text below
- Optional accent color underline separator between quote and attribution (use Tier 1/2 colors only)

## Part 3: Applying the Brand to Specific Deliverables

### Word / PowerPoint / Email (most common case)

Default styling:
- **Body text**: Times New Roman 11pt, Navy `#170055`
- **Headings (H1)**: Verdana Bold 20pt+, Navy
- **Subheads (H2/H3)**: Verdana Bold, Navy
- **Stage / section banners**: White text on Navy background bar
- **Accent rules / dividers**: Orange `#FE6500`
- **Note callouts / sidebars**: Navy text on Digital Gray `#EEEEEE` background
- **Page footer**: Small italic Verdana or Times New Roman, Navy
- **Logo placement**: Top-left or centered on cover, primary (Orange + Black) on light backgrounds

### HTML Artifacts / Web

Default CSS:
```css
:root {
  --yuu-navy: #170055;
  --yuu-orange: #FE6500;
  --yuu-digital-gray: #EEEEEE;
  --yuu-purple: #B4A1FF;
  --yuu-yellow: #E4F221;
  --yuu-blue: #008AFF;
  --yuu-red: #FF1D46;
  --yuu-pink: #FF5192;
}
body {
  font-family: 'Times New Roman', Georgia, serif;
  color: var(--yuu-navy);
  background: #FFFFFF;
}
h1, h2, h3, .heading {
  font-family: 'Verdana', sans-serif;
  font-weight: bold;
  color: var(--yuu-navy);
}
.stage-banner {
  background: var(--yuu-navy);
  color: #FFFFFF;
  padding: 12px 16px;
}
.accent-rule {
  border-bottom: 3px solid var(--yuu-orange);
}
```

For external/marketing-grade web work where licenses permit, swap Verdana → Nimbus Sans Extended and Times New Roman → Bookmania.

### Slide layouts (PowerPoint)

Common layout patterns:

- **Cover slide**: Navy background, large white wordmark, Orange Y mark in corner OR full-bleed white with centered primary logo
- **Section divider**: Solid Navy background, large white title in Verdana, optional arrow graphic
- **Content slide**: White background, Navy headline at top, body content below, Orange accent rule under the headline
- **Split layout**: 50/50 photo + content, with arrow graphic as separator or photo crop

### Sub-brand variations

If producing materials for a specific sub-brand (Alumni, Career Pathways, Talent Networks, YUPRO Placement, Grads of Life):
- Use the approved sub-brand lockup, not the standard YUU logo
- Otherwise apply the same color and typography rules as core YUU

- Don't use accent colors as body text

- Don't create new sub-brand lockups

- Apply arrows as supergraphics, photo crops, beside text, or in place of bullets
- Use the Career Pathways disambiguation note in Career Connect student-facing materials when there's risk of confusion
